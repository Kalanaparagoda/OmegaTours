Unforgettable Destinations

From ancient rock fortresses to misty mountains and golden shores, every corner of Sri Lanka tells a story.

Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Climb the ancient 'Lion Rock' fortress to witness breathtaking frescoes and panoramic views. A UNESCO World Heritage site and a true marvel of engineering.

Ella & Hill Country

Experience the heart of tea country. Hike to Ella Rock, see the iconic Nine Arch Bridge, and breathe in the fresh mountain air.

Yala National Park

Embark on a thrilling safari to spot leopards, elephants, and a stunning array of birdlife in one of the world's best wildlife destinations.

Galle Fort

Wander through the charming cobblestone streets of this Dutch colonial fort, filled with boutique shops, cafes, and rich history.

Southern Beaches

From the surfing paradise of Arugam Bay to the tranquil shores of Mirissa and Unawatuna, find your perfect spot in the sun.

Kandy

The cultural capital, home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic. Enjoy a peaceful walk around Kandy Lake and witness a traditional dance show.

A Local's Guide to Your Trip

Best Time to Visit

For the west and south coasts, the best time is from December to March. For the east coast and ancient cities, it's from April to September. But honestly, it's a beautiful destination year-round!

Getting Around

Tuk-tuks are perfect for short trips (always agree on a price first!). For longer journeys, hire a car with a driver or take our scenic trains. It's an experience in itself.

What to Pack

Light cotton clothes, swimwear, and a good pair of walking shoes. Bring a shawl or sarong for visiting temples, and a light jacket for the cool evenings in the hill country.

Respectful Travel

When visiting temples, please cover your shoulders and knees. It's a sign of respect. And always be mindful when taking photos of people or religious sites.

Stay Connected

Getting a local SIM card at the airport is easy and affordable. It's the best way to have data on the go for maps and sharing your amazing photos!

Embrace the Spice!

Don't be afraid to try the local food from small eateries. If you're not used to spicy food, you can always ask for "less spicy". We want you to enjoy it!
